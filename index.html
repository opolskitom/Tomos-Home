about

Hello! My name is Tomo (...or Thomas formally). I am a recent graduate from the University of Michigan, Dearborn with a Bachelor's in Computer Science. A lot of my time spent in college was dealing with personal struggles and since then, I have done as much as I can to both better myself for the future, and achieve my goal of becoming a successful software developer in this extremely competitive industry. What I lack in experience, I guarantee to make up for in effort and dedication, and I hope to be viewed as a competitive candidate for vital positions within a company that allows me to learn, grow, and create lasting relations with others.

My skills and technologies range from front-end design in HTML, CSS, XML in Android Studio, pyQt, and Unity, to experience in back-end languages such as C++, Python, JavaScript, SQL, and Java. I am knowledgeable with Agile methodologies such as Scrum, and am familiar with project management tools such as Git and GitHub. I am also working to become fluent in Japanese, having studied abroad to further learn the language and gain invaluable cross-cultural experiences. Currently, I am primarily searching for entry-level software, mobile, and web application developer positions to get my feet off the ground and really start contributing to large-scale, team-based projects.

projects

These are some of the projects I have worked on in my time during college and independent studying, and most can be expanded to learn more by clicking on the body text (currently unfinished!).

Pro e-Wallet

Client-based Android application which targets sales managers to allow them to track their agent's transactions and monitor the flow of expenses.

Personal Newsletter

A personal newsletter subscription webpage that allows users to sign up to receive newsletters from my personal MailChimp account, and deployed on Heroku.

YouTube QR Scraper

Python GUI application that takes in a YouTube video link or search link, parses it, and gets all QR data from the video(s) using pyQt5 and OpenCV.

Sudoku Solver

A simple, playable Sudoku solver website made with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS which uses backtracking to solve and display completed Sudoku puzzles.

TinDog

Simple BootStrap mockup Tinder website which was part of the full-stack developer course I took on Udemy to learn proper web design and responsiveness.

Slayer

A for-fun, CastleVania-inspired, 2D side-scroller created with a team of 3 people using Unity3D and their built-in version control.
